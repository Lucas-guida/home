Robotic Sampling Payload

Project to design a satellite payload system to sample material on the asteroid 3753 Cruithne. The motivation for the payload is soon other methods for mining will be exhausted and we will need to look elsewhere for these resources.The system includes a robotic arm to extend an acquisition pod to obtain surface material. I performed calculation to ensures pod will remain sealed with solenoids and to maintain motor and O-ring operating temperatures.

Blood Clot Removal

For our Microsystems course my team designed a release mechanism of anticoagulant to treat blood clots. The microsystem is inserted into the blood vessel where an external signal interacts with the dipole carbon nanotube antenna. The antenna generates a current to increase the temperature of the thermal actuator toe release the anticoagulant.

Solar Cell Deployment System

Designed a system to allow folded up solar cells to be deployed from CubeSat. The system increases the area solar cells can be applied, allowing for more solar power absorption.

3D Printer

Built a 3D-printer using Arduino MEGA micro-controller. The motors, printer extruder and LCD/SD card interface are all connected to a RAMPS 1.4 board.

Used the 3D-printer to prototype the QB50 project at York University.

Characterize Solar Cells

My team conducted an experiment to determine the maximum power point of three different solar cells due to changing characteristics of current through the semiconductor material. We used an Arduino as an analog to digital converter to obtain the data. The data was analyzed and plotted using Matlab.

PCB Environmental Test and Analysis

The purpose of this project was to compare the vibrational modes, forces and acceleration of a Printed Circuit Board (PCB). The model was simulated in Siemens NX to compare theoretical and actual test results.

After the test, we found that one of the screws holding the PCB in place became loose, which was the cause of the deviation in the blue and green curves.

Chalk Board Tracking System

The goal of this project was to create a system that would enhance the lecture environment for students. It was found that many professors at York University still make use of hand written notes on the chalk board in lecture halls.

The team created a prototype chalk-holder with IR-LED. The LED was tracked by a Wii-remote and translated live to a website for students to view on mobile devices.

Autonomous Robot

The purpose of this project was to create a robot that would navigate a course and perform a task of pushing an exercise ball at a given distance through the course.

Through the use of buttons, switches and sensors the robot was able to successfully navigate the course. Our group achieved third place out of ten teams.
