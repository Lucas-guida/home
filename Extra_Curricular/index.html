Blood Clot Removal

For our Microsystems course my team designed a release mechanism of anticoagulant to treat blood clots. The microsystem is inserted into the blood vessel where an external signal interacts with the dipole carbon nanotube antenna. The antenna generates a current to increase the temperature of the thermal actuator toe release the anticoagulant.

Solar Cell Deployment System

Designed a system to allow folded up solar cells to be deployed from CubeSat. The system increases the area solar cells can be applied, allowing for more solar power absorption.

3D Printer

Built a 3D-printer using Arduino MEGA micro-controller. The motors, printer extruder and LCD/SD card interface are all connected to a RAMPS 1.4 board.

The purpose for creating the 3D-printer is to rapid porotype future designs before production. I am currently using the 3D-printer to prototype for the QB50 project at York University.

Update: Currently in the process of upgrading printer with heated bead to improve print quality.

Characterize Solar Cells

The goal of this project was to assess the data acquisition of the Arduino UNO through measuring voltages of a circuit powered by a solar cell. By doing so, we aimed to distinguish three solar cells, varying in size and composition, through their current-voltage (I-V) curve. A quantitative comparison with online datasheets for the three solar cells will also be performed. The fill factor (FF) of the solar cells is used for comparison. This factor allowed for a comparison of how the solar cell is operating from its ideal value.

PCB Environmental Test and Analysis

The purpose of this project was to compare the vibrational modes, forces, and acceleration of a Printed Circuit Board (PCB) board produced from a simulated model to that of the actual test.

We saw that the low frequency modes are very close to the expected values, while the larger frequency values are higher. After the test we found that one of the screws holding the PCB in place became loose, which was the cause of the deviation.

Chalk Board Tracking System

The goal of this project was to create a system that would enhance the lecture environment for students. It was found that many professors at York University still make use of hand written notes on the chalk board in lecture halls with 300+ students. This made it difficult to read for students at the back of the lecture hall.With our custom chalk holder, we were able to track the writing pattern on the board and live stream it to a website where students could view the notes on their mobile devices.

Autonomous Robot

Through the use of buttons, switches and sensors the robot was able to successfully navigate the course. Our group achieved third place out of ten teams.
